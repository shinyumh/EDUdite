What is flexbox?

Flexbox, an element of CSS, ensures that the layout and distribution between every element on a webpage!

How does it work?

  1. Declare parent functions with display: flex; to transform the element into a flex container
  2. Call any of these properties:
    1. justify-content - horizontal alignment
      • justify-content: center; (center of the container)
      • justify-content: flex-start; (beginning of the container)
      • justify-content: flex-end; (end of the container)
      • justify-content: space-around; (before, between, or after lines)
      • justify-content: space-between; (space between lines)
    2. flex-direction - direction
      • flex-direction: column; (top to bottom)
      • flex-direction: column-reverse; (bototm to top)
      • flex-direction: row; (left to right)
      • flex-direction: row-reverse (right to left)
    3. flex-wrap - whether or not items should wrap on one line
      • flex-wrap: wrap; (wrap elements)
      • flex-wrap: nowrap; (doesn't wrap elements)
      • flex-wrap: wrap-reverse; (wrap in reverse order)
    4. flex-flow - flex-direction and flex-wrap!
      • flex-flow: row wrap;
    5. align-items - vertical alignment
      • align-items: center; (middle of the container)
      • align-items: flex-start; (top of the container)
      • align-items: flex-end; (bottom of the container)
      • align-items: stretch (fill the container)
      • align-items: baseline (align with baselines)
    6. align-content - flex line alignment
      • align-content: space-between; (equal space between each element)
      • align-content: space-around; (space before, between, and after)
      • align-content: stretch; (take up remaining space)
      • align-content: center; (middle of container)
      • align-content: flex-start (start of each container)
      • align-content: flex-end (end of each container)
